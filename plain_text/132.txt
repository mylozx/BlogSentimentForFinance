Raises $172.5m in a Debenture Offering Led by BMO Capital Markets By: Marketwire . Jan. 13, 2011 05:05 PM VANCOUVER, BRITISH COLUMBIA -- (Marketwire) -- 01/13/11 -- In this week's report, the CFB published term sheets for 15 new proposed placements from the mining, oil/gas (termed metals and energy in the report) and technology sectors. Of those, 11 were for mining stocks, three for oil/gas stocks, and one for technology stocks with the total value of new proposals reaching over $43.7m. One of these placements was designated a 'flow through' issuance and there were no new debenture offerings. The largest new public proposal was by Scorpio Gold Corp. (SGN.TO), which launched an offering consisting of 18m shares priced at $0.60 for proceeds of $10.8m in a placement led by Jennings Capital. The CFB published term sheets for 63 placements that were closed during the week. Of these, 44 were for mining stocks, 11 for oil/gas, and eight for technology stocks, with the total value of these closings being over $484m. 16 of these placements were designated 'flow through' issuances and one new debenture placement closed. The largest public closing was by Rainy River Resources Ltd. (RR.TO) which issued 5.93m shares (including a 790,000 share overallotment) at a price of $12.65 for gross proceeds of $75.0m in a placement led by Scotia Capital. The CFB also tracked four amendments to placements and four overallotments, published at the end of the weekly report. To date, there have been 328 weekly reports created by CFB; backdated reports can be obtained by subscribers.
